What are the things that you need to do first upon seeing such animals?

The presence of the king of the wild may be felt in your neighborhood's streets. Because humans are destroying their natural environment, the stray lion appears to be distressed and on the lookout for a new home. Running is a bad choice since you won't be able to outrun a lion. First and foremost, do not flee. Resist the temptation to flee since the huge cat may respond with a "chase, catch, and kill" response. When you see a lion, don't approach it; instead, gently walk away from it without turning your back on it.

You're thinking about ways to relax and forget about your problems, so you've decided to go trekking and camping. You start to hear some animal sounds around halfway through your trip, but you have no idea what it is until you come across a herd of elephants. They appear nice in your eyes, but you forget you're in the jungle, not the circus. Elephant assaults claim the lives of around 500 people each year. It's about charge if the elephant's ears are pinned back and the trunks are curled inward. Always keep your back to the elephant and begin sprinting in a zig-zag motion. Find a tree or something else to keep you and the charging elephant apart. You may either climb the tree and wait for rescue, or you can create a loud noise, such as yelling, to scare it away. Elephants dislike loud noises.

ELEPHANT

You're camping with your pals beside a river. Then, all of a sudden, there's a big splash of water. Then you and your pals are being watched by a crocodile. You had intended to swim in the river, but now the crocodiles have arrived. This appears to be an impossibility right now. Crocodiles are currently regarded the big animal responsible for the most human deaths in Africa, with an estimated 1000 deaths each year, according to the United Nations' Food and Agricultural Organization, however exact figures are difficult to come by. Things are now given since crocodiles are in the river; do not swim in crocodile-infested waters, avoid swimming in the river at night, crocodiles are surprisingly fast, so stay away, avoid camping near waters, and avoid feeding the crocodiles because you might get eaten instead of the food you're giving.

CROCODILE

It's crucial to know how to avoid being attacked by a 1,500-pound grizzly bear before knowing how to defend yourself. When a mother grizzly bear defends her offspring, the majority of grizzly bear assaults occur. So, if you encounter a bear cub, get out of there swiftly and silently. To be safe, avoid grizzly-infested regions during the months of March and April, when juvenile grizzlies are likely to start roaming for the first time. However, if someone does attack, fight the impulse to flee. Experts recommend that individuals blow up their chests and keep totally motionless instead. If it doesn't work, individuals are instructed to lie down on their bellies, shield their necks with their hands, and pretend to be dead.

BEAR

Shark attacks, as rare as they may be, are among some people's greatest concerns. Many people would try to swim away instinctively if a shark approached. Even Michael Phelps, on the other hand, would not be able to outswim a shark. So, what should ordinary people do if they aren't Olympic-level athletes? Rather than turning away and swimming, attempt to remain motionless and prevent splashing (it would be pretty difficult, we know). Simply float in the water and pretend to be dead. If it doesn't work, try punching. Sharks are particularly sensitive in the area between their eyes and on their nose.

How to handle wild animals?

Noise and excessive movement are perceived as dangers by wild animals, particularly prey species. Animals should be handled as quickly as possible. Reduce the amount of time spent in hand with animals since they grow stressed when they are near humans. Allowing animals to be used for "show and tell" to neighbors and friends is not a good idea.
